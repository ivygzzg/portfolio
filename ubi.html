Context

What is UBI?

UBI is a groundbreaking app that, in combination with a cutting-edge ubiSensor, transforms any car into a smart vehicle. By providing drivers with actionable insights, the app empowers them to improve their driving habits, qualify for insurance discounts through safe driving, monitor their vehicle's health in real-time, and access roadside assistance during breakdowns.

My team and I were given the task of researching the main screens of the product, identifying pain points, and developing recommendations to revamp the app. We ended up redesigning the main screens, giving users confidence to navigate.

What is an ubiSensor?

A device that retrieves key data from your vehicle through a telematic tracking system. UBI app then displays the data in neat, sleek infographics to help users review their driving skills — and get additional benefits from their insurance.

Problem

Drivers face challenges that keep them from a smooth experience

Accessing driving information

Users struggle to find or understand essential driving data presented by the app.

Lack of awareness of benefits

Many users are unaware of the benefits their insurance provides due to complex or inaccessible information.

Unclear emergency assistance

The process of requesting help during accidents or mishaps is not intuitive, leading to confusion and delays in critical situations.

Objective

The primary objective was to redesign the main screen, improve navigation, and introduce new functions to improve the user experience

We focused on maintaining a solid product design while facilitating immediate access to information — making it effortless for users to view their data at a glance.

The redesign maintained brand consistency while significantly improving usability, navigation clarity, and awareness of insurance benefits.

Research & Insights

Understanding the weaknesses of the former interface

In the initial research phase, we analyzed the existing app and obtained all the general documentation of the project. We spent time with key stakeholders to fully comprehend their expectations, needs, and vision of the product.

The central research question became:

Research Question

How can we promote better driving habits through the app?

By detecting drivers who use the app and those already registered, I was able to find weak points and understand the biggest problems users faced.

We mapped out findings in a simple matrix to highlight ongoing issues vs. actual behavior — a quick way to generate ideas and potential solutions for the ideation phase.

Behavior Matrix — Miro board

Solution & User Flow

Working closely with the team to define the solution

Collecting insights from initial research findings, we identified four core solution areas.

01

Let the user know all the details of every benefit their insurance provides.

02

Improve the request for assistance directly through a shortcut in case of any emergency.

03

Re-order sections for a more intuitive navigation flow.

04

Improve the score visualization based on their last trips of the vehicle.

Information architecture mapping out the full app structure — from Home through Trip detail, Benefits, and bottom navigation.

Design Execution

Iterating on layouts, icons, and visual elements to reach high-fidelity

In this phase we experimented extensively with layouts, icons, background colors, and shadows to achieve the desired outcome — then moved into high-fidelity prototyping.

Home Screen

Dashboard — the control center

On the main screen, we reflected a driving score of the last two weeks based on the driver's trips. We added the last trip tab and a section with benefits.

A shortcut to call their insurance was added as a sticky button. The navigation tab was rearranged to My Car, Insurance, and Notifications — grouping all necessary information logically.

At the top, users can access their account profile and a button for roadside assistance — immediately accessible in any emergency.

Home screen — Hi-fi

Trip Detail

Accurate tracking, immediate clarity

Users have access to detailed data on their most recent trip via a shortcut on the home screen. UBI shows drivers an accurate tracking map of their travel, as well as the score they obtained based on acceleration, braking, speed, and driving behavior.

Benefits

Making insurance benefits visible

Insured driver's benefits are displayed directly on the home screen — a somewhat obvious solution that dramatically improves ease of use. Now UBI users can easily acknowledge the benefits of good driving.

Each benefit has a dedicated detail screen with full description and required information, plus direct Chat and Call actions.
