Case Study · Mobile · Fintech

Integrating loyalty coupons into Velpay's payment flow

ProjectCupones Lealtad · Velpay
RoleUX/UI Designer
TeamEngineer team · Scrum Master
Scope1 month · Sierra Madre pilot

Context

Turning the POS into a loyalty tool

Velpay needed to enable loyalty program benefits — specifically coupons — directly inside the payment flow of its TPV (point of sale terminal). The goal was to allow cashiers to apply coupon codes during a sale, validate them in real time against the merchant's loyalty system, and reflect the discount before processing the payment.

The first pilot was with Sierra Madre, a merchant with an existing loyalty program. The challenge was integrating their external coupon system into Velpay's flow without disrupting payments, conciliation, or liquidation.

Objective

Enable loyalty program benefits (coupons) in the TPV, allowing merchants to incentivize purchases without affecting the standard payment operation, conciliation, or liquidation processes.

Strategy: Diferenciador — this feature positions Velpay beyond a payment processor into a merchant value tool, reducing churn and enabling new commercial opportunities.

Problem

Merchants had no way to apply loyalty benefits at the point of sale

Loyalty programs lived outside the payment flow

Merchants using loyalty systems had no way to apply discounts inside Velpay's TPV — cashiers had to manage them manually, creating errors and inconsistencies.

No real-time validation

Without API integration, there was no way to verify if a coupon was valid, already used, or blocked — opening the door to fraud and misuse.

Discount application risked payment integrity

Applying a discount incorrectly could affect the amount sent to the acquirer, impacting conciliation and liquidation. The solution had to be airtight.

No traceability of applied benefits

There was no record linking applied coupons to transactions, making audits, reversals, and support cases nearly impossible to resolve.

Design Constraints

Complex business rules that shaped every decision

Every UX decision in this project was driven by specific business and financial rules. Getting these wrong would break payment integrity.

Payment floor

Minimum $100 after discount

After applying all coupons, the card payment must be at least $100. Tips are excluded from this calculation and cannot be discounted.

Coupon behavior

100% redemption, no leftover balance

Coupons are redeemed fully — even if they exceed the purchase total. No remaining balance is carried forward.

Stacking

Multiple coupons per transaction

More than one coupon can be applied in a single sale, as long as the $100 minimum floor is maintained after all discounts.

Failure handling

Loyalty failure never blocks payment

If the loyalty system is unavailable or returns an error, the payment flow continues at full price. Coupons never block a sale.

Confirmation logic

Benefits confirmed only on successful payment

Coupon redemption is only confirmed after a successful transaction. Rejections, reversals, or cancellations trigger a benefit reversal to the merchant's system.

Exclusion

Tips and MSI cannot be combined with coupons

Tips are excluded from discount calculations. MSI (installments) and coupon discounts cannot be active simultaneously for fixed-amount transactions.

Solution — User Flow

A new step in the checkout — invisible when not needed

The loyalty coupon screen is only shown when the merchant has the feature enabled. It appears between the cart and the payment method screen — a natural insertion that doesn't disrupt the existing flow when coupons aren't used.

Two input methods reduce friction for both cashiers and customers: scanning a QR code directly, or entering the code manually. The running total updates in real time as coupons are applied, so there's no ambiguity about the final amount before processing.

01
Cashier initiates sale in TPV

Total is calculated. If the merchant has loyalty enabled, the system surfaces the "Agregar cupones" screen before payment method selection.

02
Customer provides coupon — QR scan or manual code

Cashier scans QR or enters code manually. System validates in real time against the merchant's loyalty API. Minimum floor ($100) is always visible to prevent invalid configurations.

03
Discount applied and reflected immediately

Valid coupon appears in the list with its discount amount in green. Total updates instantly. Multiple coupons can be stacked. Cashier can remove any coupon with the × button.

04
Payment processed at discounted amount

The discounted total is sent to the acquirer — not the original price. Ticket reflects: consumo, propina, descuento(s), and total cobrado. Benefit is confirmed only on payment success.

05
Reversal if payment fails

If the payment is rejected, cancelled, or reversed, the loyalty system is notified to revert the coupon redemption automatically — no manual intervention needed.

Design Execution

Clarity at the moment of payment — every state designed

Three key screens cover the full coupon experience: empty state (no coupons yet), applied state (discounts reflected), and error state (invalid or used coupon).

Empty state — no coupons yet

Applied — 3 coupons, -$300 discount

Error state — cupón inválido o ya utilizado

Error — invalid or used coupon

Empty state — setting the stage

The QR scan area is the primary action — large, tappable, and immediately clear. Manual entry is secondary. The running total is already visible even with zero coupons, showing "Mínimo a pagar: $100" as a persistent constraint reminder for the cashier.

Applied state — real-time feedback

Each applied coupon appears as a row with its code and discount in green. The × removes it instantly. The summary section updates live — Consumo, Propina (explicitly marked as non-discountable), Descuento total in green, and bold Total a pagar. No ambiguity before hitting Continuar.

Before vs. After

From manual and disconnected to real-time and traceable

Before

No loyalty integration — discounts applied manually outside the TPV
No real-time coupon validation — fraud and reuse possible
Discounts not reflected in the amount sent to the acquirer
No traceability between coupons and transactions
Reversal of benefits required manual coordination

After

Coupons applied directly in the TPV payment flow
Real-time validation via merchant's loyalty API
Discounted amount sent to acquirer — payment integrity preserved
Full audit trail: coupon ID, discount, transaction ID, status
Automatic benefit reversal on failed or cancelled payments

Learnings

Designing around constraints is the skill

This project had more business rules than most — minimum floors, stacking logic, tip exclusions, reversal triggers. Each rule had a UX implication: what to show, when to block, how to communicate a constraint without stopping the sale.

The biggest design decision was making the loyalty flow feel optional and frictionless. If the merchant doesn't use coupons, the screen is invisible. If the loyalty system fails, the payment continues. The feature adds value without ever becoming a blocker.

Traceability was also a UX concern — the ticket redesign to show Consumo / Propina / Descuento / Total had to be clear enough for both the cashier and the end customer, while also serving as the audit record for Operations.

Good POS UX means invisible complexity — the cashier sees simplicity, the system handles everything else.

Integrating loyalty coupons into Velpay's TPV turned a manual, disconnected process into a real-time, traceable feature — without touching the existing payment flow. The result positions Velpay as more than a payment processor: a platform that helps merchants retain customers.
