Context

What is Lealtad Cupones?

Lealtad Cupones is a feature integration for Velpay's TPV (Point of Sale terminal) that allows merchants to offer and apply loyalty coupon benefits directly within the payment flow.

The goal was to enable merchants to incentivize purchases without disrupting the standard payment operation, reconciliation, or settlement processes — keeping the experience seamless for both cashiers and end customers.

Project objective

Enable loyalty benefit application (coupons) within the TPV, allowing merchants to incentivize purchases without affecting payment operations, reconciliation, or settlement.

Problem

Merchants need to reward customers without breaking the payment flow

No loyalty touchpoint at checkout

Merchants had no native way to apply coupon or loyalty benefits at the point of sale — missing a key moment to retain customers.

Risk of disrupting payment operations

Any new feature added to the TPV flow could potentially break reconciliation and settlement processes if not designed carefully.

Coupon validation complexity

Coupons needed real-time validation against an external loyalty system, handling eligibility, expiration, and benefit rules without slowing down the cashier.

Coupon status traceability

There was no clear mechanism to revert a coupon benefit in case of a failed payment, rejected transaction, or cancellation.

Objective

Design a coupon redemption experience that fits naturally into the checkout flow without adding friction

The coupon entry and validation had to happen before the payment step, giving the cashier full visibility of the discounted amount before charging the customer's card.

The integration also needed to ensure the loyalty system was notified of confirmations, reversals, and cancellations — tying coupon status directly to payment outcomes.

Scope & Constraints

Defining what's in and out for Phase 1

The first phase focused exclusively on Sierra Madre as the pilot merchant. The scope was deliberately scoped to coupon redemption within the card payment method — not loyalty card programs or other payment methods.

Key business rule: the system must always send a minimum of $100 MXN to the acquirer after any coupon discount, regardless of the coupon value.

Business rules

Minimum charge At least $100 MXN must be sent to the acquirer after discount (tip excluded).
Full redemption Coupons are redeemed 100% — no remaining balance if they exceed the purchase amount.
Multiple coupons More than one coupon can be applied to a single transaction.
Status check Before redeeming, the app verifies the coupon is available and not blocked by an in-progress transaction.

User Flow & Architecture

Mapping the checkout journey with coupons

The coupon step is introduced before the payment method selection. Once the cashier enters a coupon (manually or via QR), the system validates it in real time against the merchant's loyalty system.

If valid, the discount is immediately reflected in the total. If the loyalty system is unavailable, the flow continues with the original amount — never blocking the sale.

API Integration

Designing around three core API services

Working closely with the engineering team, I helped define the interaction model for three API endpoints that power the entire coupon lifecycle within the TPV flow.

Each service maps directly to a user action — query on entry, redeem on payment success, cancel on failure or reversal.

Consulta de descuentos

Called when the cashier enters a coupon. Returns validity, eligibility, discount amount, and availability status.

Redención de descuentos

Called after a successful payment. Confirms the coupon has been used and updates its status in the loyalty system.

Cancelación de descuentos

Called on payment rejection, reversal, or cancellation. Sends the transaction ID to revert the benefit and free up the coupon.

Design Execution

Designing clear states for every coupon scenario

The UI needed to communicate clearly across multiple states: coupon entry, valid discount applied, already redeemed, validation error, and system unavailable — each with different actions available to the cashier.

Coupon Entry

Manual input & QR scan

The coupon entry component appears within the checkout flow when loyalty is enabled for the merchant. The cashier can type the code manually or use the device camera for QR scanning.

The input is validated in real time — the cashier sees immediate feedback before proceeding, so there's no ambiguity about whether the coupon was accepted or not.

Coupon entry screen

Discount Applied

Immediate reflection on the total

Once validated, the discount appears clearly in the order summary before the cashier charges the customer. The updated ticket shows: subtotal, tip (not subject to coupon), discount amount, coupon details, and final total.

This gives the cashier full confidence in what will be charged — and what will be printed on the customer receipt.

Discount applied — order summary

Error States

Handling every failure gracefully

A critical design requirement: the loyalty feature must never block the payment flow. Each error state shows a clear message and always offers a path forward — either try another coupon or proceed with full amount.

Updated Receipt

Full transparency on the printed ticket

The ticket was redesigned to clearly show all coupon-related information: subtotal, tip, discount applied, coupon identifier, and the final amount charged to the card.

The final amount (post-discount) is what gets sent to the acquirer, authorized, and reconciled — maintaining full integrity of the payment pipeline.

Impact & Outcomes

Enabling merchants to reward customers at the right moment

By embedding loyalty coupon redemption directly into the TPV checkout flow, merchants like Sierra Madre can now apply benefits at the moment of purchase — increasing frequency of visits, average ticket, and customer retention.

The full coupon lifecycle (query → redeem → cancel) is tied to payment outcomes, ensuring the loyalty system stays in sync with actual transaction results.

For merchants Increased purchase frequency and average ticket by offering benefits at point of sale.
For the platform TPV becomes a value-added tool — differentiating Velpay from standard payment processors.
For operations Full traceability and audit log of all applied benefits, enabling easy reconciliation.
